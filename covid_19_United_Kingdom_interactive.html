COVID-19 IN THE UNITED KINGDOM

Cases


The number of diagnosed cases per million people is comparable in all countries of the United Kingdom.
It is worth noticing that the population density of England is higher than the other three countries.
This may indicate that England was very effective in preventing the disease or that population density does not affect the number of cases.
We can also see that the highest number of cases was found for women between 35 and 39 years old.

Tests


The highest number of PCR tests per 100 people was performed in England.
The other 3 UK countries had significantly lower number of PCR tests per 100 people.
Despite the higher number of PCR tests performed, the number of diagnosed cases per million people in England was comparable to the other UK countries.
This may confirm that England was more effective in preventing the disease than other UK countries.

Deaths


The highest number of deaths among people diagnosed with COVID-19 has been reported in England.
Although England appears to have been the most successful in preventing infection, this country had the lowest efficiency in treating the disease.

The impact of vaccinations


From the plots presented, it can be seen that vaccination has not effectively inhibited the emergence of new COVID-19 cases.
However, after the first wave of vaccination (spring-summer 2021), the number of deaths during the next wave of COVID-19 disease (winter 2021/2022)
was significantly lower than in previous waves of disease.
This may confirm that vaccination does not prevent infection, but mitigates the course of the disease.

Impact of the pandemic on other aspects of life


The pandemic has taken its toll on people in other aspects of life as well.
The public's anxiety level increased significantly during lockdowns (successive waves of COVID-19) and declined markedly during spring and summer periods.
The highest levels of anxiety were observed among young people.
The pandemic also increased unemployment among both men and women.

Comparison to Poland


The course of the pandemic in the UK and Poland was similar.
It can be noted that at the beginning of the pandemic there were significantly more deaths per million people in the UK than in Poland.
During the winter 2021/2022 disease wave, the UK had a higher number of infections per million people.
Despite this, the UK had a greater success rate in treating the disease/preventing its severity than Poland,
what is confirmed by the lower number of deaths per million people in the UK.
